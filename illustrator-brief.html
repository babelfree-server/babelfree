01 The Project

What you are building and why it matters

El Viaje del Jaguar is an interactive language-learning game set in the ecosystems of Colombia. A female jaguar-spirit named Yaguará travels through rainforest, Andes, llanos, desert, islands, snowy peaks, and Caribbean coast, meeting allies and facing forces of forgetting that are erasing the world's names. The game spans 89 destinations, 20,901 games across 8 ecosystems, from CEFR A1 to C2.

Your job is not to illustrate hundreds of scenes. Instead, you are building a visual compositing toolkit — a set of modular layers (backgrounds, character sprites, icon objects, mood overlays) that the game engine combines at runtime to create thousands of unique screen compositions. Think of it as building a puppet theater: you paint the backdrops, carve the puppets, and craft the props. The engine moves them on stage.

The visual world draws from Colombian indigenous art, textile patterns, and natural landscapes. It is NOT Disney, manga, or generic fantasy. These are spirits, not cartoon animals. This world is alive, warm, and ancient.

"No todas las palabras se dicen. Algunas se cuidan."

The Four Layers

1
8 Ecosystem Backgrounds — Panoramic landscapes of Colombia (bosque, costa, desierto, islas, llanos, nevada, selva, sierra). These repeat across destinations sharing the same ecosystem.
2
5 Character Sprites × 3 poses = 15 illustrations — Yaguará, Candelaria, Río, Doña Asunción, Don Próspero. Each with transparent background, placed dynamically by the engine.
3
25 Icon Objects — Game props and UI icons: books, keys, frogs, scrolls, compasses, musical notes. Small illustrated objects used across all game types.
4
3 World Mood Overlays — Color/light filters that transform any background into Mundo de Abajo (green dawn), Mundo del Medio (amber golden hour), or Mundo de Arriba (purple twilight).

Total Deliverables: 51 unique illustrations (~100 files with size variants)

Visual Identity Keywords

Warm, earthy, alive Textile-inspired Organic shapes Circles & spirals Illustrated, not photorealistic Golden-hour light Mola patterns (Kuna) Wayúu woven textures Pre-Columbian gold motifs

What this is NOT

No Disney/Pixar style No anime/manga No generic fantasy No photorealistic 3D No clip art or flat corporate No AI-generated aesthetic No hard geometric edges No harsh/fluorescent light

02 The Three Worlds

Visual atmosphere and palette for each world

The game takes place across three visual worlds. Each has a distinct color palette, mood, and landscape. As the story progresses, the world shifts from intimate jungle to open terrain to cosmic sky. The mood overlays (Layer 4) transform the ecosystem backgrounds to match each world.

El Mundo de Abajo

Jungle, river, night, roots.
Dense Chocó rainforest. Filtered green-gold light. Massive ceiba trees, buttress roots, rivers, bioluminescent moss. Warm and intimate — small spaces, close encounters. Fireflies, golden frogs, the sound of water everywhere.

Palette: deep greens, river blues, earth browns, night purples, gold accents.

El Mundo del Medio

Villages, mountains, plains, coast.
Colombia's four ecosystems in full. Chocó stilt-houses, Andean páramo, Orinoco grasslands, Caribbean shore. Human spaces mixed with nature — markets, paths, boats, abandoned buildings reclaimed by vines. Wider compositions, more characters, more complexity.

Palette: terracotta, ochre, mountain grey-blue, llanos gold, Caribbean turquoise.

El Mundo de Arriba

Stars, silence, light, transformation.
Cosmic and metaphysical. No solid walls — boundaries defined by constellations. Floors made of maps, ceilings made of sky. Luminous outlines rather than solid surfaces. Think: inside an astrolabe, a constellation chart come alive.

Palette: deep indigo, starlight silver, luminous amber, white-gold, cosmic black.

03 Characters

Layer 2 — 5 characters × 3 poses = 15 sprite illustrations

Each character is delivered as a standalone sprite on a transparent background, 800px tall. The game engine positions them over the ecosystem backgrounds. Each character needs three poses that cover their emotional range. These are not scene illustrations — they are versatile sprites that work across many contexts.

Yaguará
The Protagonist — Female Jaguar Spirit — Priority 1

Yaguará is a female jaguar-spirit (Panthera onca). NOT orange like a tiger — study real jaguars. She grows across the game from cub to elder, but for the sprite system we need one design that reads across all contexts — her Caminante (young adult) form, which is the most versatile.

Attribute Description
Build Lean, athletic, fast — a young adult in her prime. Powerful but graceful.
Fur Golden-dark with sharp, clearly defined rosettes. Deep gold-amber tones.
Eyes Green-amber, focused — the most important feature. They carry the whole story.
Constellation mark Clear pattern on left shoulder, occasional soft glow.
Scars / marks Thin scratch on her flank — a journey scar that shows she has been tested.

3 Poses Required

  • Neutral / standing — relaxed but alert, 3/4 view facing right. Ears forward, eyes engaged. The "default" sprite for dialogue and narration.
  • Happy / joyful — eyes bright, relaxed posture, almost smiling. Slight bounce. Used for victories, discoveries, positive feedback.
  • Serious / determined — eyes focused, jaw set, body low. Protective stance. Used for challenges, warnings, tense moments.
Deliver: 3 poses, 800px tall, PNG transparent background + source file with layers

04 Ecosystem Backgrounds

Layer 1 — 8 panoramic backgrounds that define the world

Each ecosystem needs two orientations of the same illustration. These backgrounds repeat across all destinations within that ecosystem — a student in dest1 (selva) and dest44 (selva) sees the same lush background. The mood overlay (Layer 4) transforms the light and color to match the world (Abajo, Medio, Arriba).

1920 × 1080 px
Landscape (desktop screens)
1080 × 1920 px
Portrait (mobile screens)

Style: Rich, atmospheric, painterly. Each must evoke the REAL Colombian landscape. No characters in the backgrounds — those are composited on top. Leave natural "character zones" (clear foreground areas where sprites can be placed).

1. Selva (Chocó / Amazon)

green, water, rain, drums — Afro-Colombian & indigenous cultures
  • Dense canopy, filtered green light, river visible below with Victoria regia lily pads
  • Macaws in the canopy, massive buttress roots, bioluminescent moss on trunks
  • This is Yaguará's HOME — it should feel warm, safe, and alive
  • Character zone: Mossy clearing in the foreground, between root formations

2. Bosque (Cloud Forest / Andean Forest)

moss, mist, vertical green, bird calls — Transition between selva and Andes
  • Cloud forest: dense, vertical, dripping with epiphytes, orchids, bromeliads, hanging moss
  • Filtered light through mist — everything is soft-edged, almost dreamlike
  • Tree ferns, narrow muddy paths, hidden waterfalls
  • Character zone: Path clearing with ferns, mist parting to create depth

3. Costa (Caribbean)

turquoise, salt, cumbia, intense heat — Afro-Caribbean culture
  • Turquoise water, white sand, palm trees, fishing boats (small, colorful, wooden)
  • Caribbean energy, intense saturated light, cumbia rhythm in the air
  • Feeling: vibrant, rhythmic, warm. Life at the edge of land and sea.
  • Character zone: Sandy beach foreground with driftwood, water behind

4. Desierto (Tatacoa / La Guajira)

ochre, cracked earth, stars, heat shimmer — Wayúu territory
  • Ochre sand, cacti, Wayúu rancherías in the distance, harsh sun beating down
  • Extreme light contrast: blazing sun, heat shimmer on the horizon
  • Feeling: exposed, ancient, elemental. The silence here is visible.
  • Character zone: Flat cracked earth in foreground, formations rising behind

5. Islas (San Andrés / Providencia / Rosario)

crystal water, coral, reggae, Raizal culture — Caribbean archipelago
  • Seven-color sea, coral visible through crystal water, wooden houses on stilts
  • White sand, coconut palms, underwater world suggested below the surface
  • Feeling: paradise with depth. The surface beauty hides an ecosystem fighting to survive.
  • Character zone: Wooden dock or white sand, palms framing the sides

6. Llanos (Orinoco Plains)

gold, open sky, harp, endless horizon — Llanero culture
  • Endless grass, cattle in the distance, dramatic sky (2/3 of the image), one tree on the horizon
  • River cutting through the plain, heat shimmer, scattered moriche palms
  • Feeling: freedom, exposure, nowhere to hide. The opposite of the sheltered forest.
  • Character zone: Riverbank in foreground, grassland stretching behind

7. Nevada / Sierra (Snowy Peaks / High Mountains)

white, wind, sacred, extreme altitude — Sierra Nevada de Santa Marta, Cocuy
  • Snow peaks rising above jungle below, mist between altitude zones, frailejones at the tree line
  • Extreme clarity of air — everything is sharp, cold, crystalline. Colors saturated against white.
  • Sacred geography: the Kogi and Arhuaco consider these peaks the heart of the world
  • Character zone: Rocky ledge in foreground, peaks and clouds behind

8. Sierra (Andean Highlands)

earth, stone, cold clear light, wind instruments — Highland communities
  • Green valleys, coffee terraces on steep slopes, colonial towns with whitewashed walls and tile roofs
  • Cold clear light, long shadows, clouds below the viewer (condor territory)
  • Feeling: ancient, vast, quiet. You can hear the wind.
  • Character zone: Stone terrace in foreground, valley depth behind
Deliver: 8 backgrounds × 2 orientations = 16 files, PNG, source files with layers

08 Style References

Show these to the illustrator — this is the visual universe we live in

DO Reference

  • Mola textile art (Kuna/Guna people) — layered color, bold organic shapes

DO NOT Reference

  • Generic "fantasy game" aesthetic — no elves, no medieval, no dark-fantasy
  • Photorealistic 3D renders — we want illustration, not simulation
  • Clip art / flat corporate — no "startup illustration" style
  • AI-generated imagery aesthetic — no Midjourney / DALL-E look

11 How the Engine Composites

Understanding how your artwork becomes a living game

The game engine (yaguara-engine.js) combines your four layers at runtime to create unique screen compositions for each of the 20,901 games. Here is how the layers stack:

Step 1: The engine selects the ecosystem background based on the current destination. A student playing in dest1 (selva ecosystem) sees bg-selva-landscape.png. A student in dest34 (costa ecosystem) sees bg-costa-landscape.png.

Step 2: The mood overlay is applied on top using CSS mix-blend-mode: multiply. Dest1 is in Mundo de Abajo (A1), so the green dawn overlay transforms the selva background. Dest34 is in Mundo del Medio (B1), so the amber golden-hour overlay warms the costa background.

Step 3: Character sprites are placed in the foreground "character zone" of the background. Yaguará might stand on the left, Candelaria on the right. The engine picks poses based on narrative context (happy after a win, serious before a challenge).

Step 4: Icon objects appear contextually — a key icon for escape rooms, a scroll for riddles, a music note for rhythm games. They float near the relevant game element or appear in the UI bar.

What This Means for You

  • Backgrounds must work with any character on top. Leave clear foreground space. Avoid busy detail in the lower third where sprites will be placed.
  • Character sprites must work on any background. Clean edges, consistent lighting from upper-left, no ground shadows baked in (the engine adds those).
  • Overlays must be subtle enough to enhance, not obliterate. Test your overlays on multiply blend mode over the backgrounds — the original landscape should remain recognizable.
  • Icons must read at both 128px and 256px. Keep shapes simple and silhouettes clear. They will appear at small sizes in the game UI.

The Result

With just 51 unique illustrations, the engine generates thousands of distinct visual compositions. A selva background + green overlay + Yaguará (happy) + book icon looks completely different from a desierto background + amber overlay + Don Próspero (scheming) + key icon. Your artwork is modular, reusable, and endlessly combinable — like a visual vocabulary that the engine uses to tell stories.
