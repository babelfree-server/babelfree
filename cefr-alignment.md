# CEFR & INSTITUTIONAL ALIGNMENT DOCUMENT
# PROJECT: LA AVENTURA DE YAGUARÁ
# PURPOSE: Explicitly map design philosophy and game mechanics to CEFR standards
# AUDIENCE: AI system (Claude), institutions, curriculum designers, evaluators

#####################################################################
# 0. POSITIONING STATEMENT
#####################################################################

La Aventura de Yaguará is a game-based, story-driven Spanish learning
system aligned with the Common European Framework of Reference for
Languages (CEFR).

The system does NOT replace CEFR.
It operationalizes CEFR through experiential, action-based learning.

CEFR levels are treated as:
- Descriptors of communicative ability
- Not as content containers
- Not as grammar checklists

#####################################################################
# 1. OVERALL ALIGNMENT STRATEGY
#####################################################################

CEFR alignment is achieved through:

- Action-oriented tasks (CEFR core principle)
- Can-do statements mapped to game actions
- Increasing communicative complexity across spirals
- Embedded assessment via meaningful interaction

CEFR levels are:
- Structurally present
- Pedagogically respected
- Intentionally invisible to the learner

#####################################################################
# 2. CEFR PRINCIPLES ADOPTED EXPLICITLY
#####################################################################

The system aligns with the following CEFR principles:

- Language as social action
- Learners as social agents
- Tasks as central unit of learning
- Competence over content coverage
- Progressive autonomy

These principles directly inform:
- Game archetypes
- Fibonacci progression
- Assessment logic

#####################################################################
# 3. CEFR LEVEL ALIGNMENT BY SPIRAL
#####################################################################

-------------------------------------------------------------
SPIRAL 1 — A1 (Breakthrough)
-------------------------------------------------------------

CEFR FOCUS:
- Basic interaction
- Recognition and production of familiar words
- Simple phrases related to immediate environment

CEFR CAN-DO ALIGNMENT:
- Can recognize familiar words and very basic phrases
- Can use simple words to identify objects
- Can understand slow, clear speech with visual support

GAME ACTIONS:
- Naming Rituals
- Guided Movement (simple imperatives)

INSTITUTIONAL LANGUAGE:
Learners demonstrate A1 competence through successful
completion of contextualized naming and comprehension tasks
in supported environments.

-------------------------------------------------------------
SPIRAL 2 — A2 (Waystage)
-------------------------------------------------------------

CEFR FOCUS:
- Simple exchanges
- Routine tasks requiring direct exchange of information
- Description of immediate environment and basic needs

CEFR CAN-DO ALIGNMENT:
- Can communicate in simple and routine tasks
- Can describe in simple terms aspects of background and immediate environment
- Can handle short social exchanges

GAME ACTIONS:
- Naming Ritual (expanded vocabulary)
- Guided Movement (expanded instructions)
- Story Weaving (very simple sequencing)

INSTITUTIONAL LANGUAGE:
Learners demonstrate A2 competence through participation
in routine communicative exchanges and basic descriptive
tasks within familiar game-world contexts.

-------------------------------------------------------------
SPIRAL 3 — B1 (Threshold)
-------------------------------------------------------------

CEFR FOCUS:
- Narration of events and experiences
- Description of feelings, reactions, and opinions
- Handling most situations likely to arise while traveling

CEFR CAN-DO ALIGNMENT:
- Can connect phrases in a simple way to describe experiences and events
- Can narrate a story or relate the plot of a book or film
- Can briefly give reasons and explanations for opinions and plans

GAME ACTIONS:
- Story Weaving (past tense narration)
- Guided Movement with narrated intent
- Interaction with environment through time (before / after)

INSTITUTIONAL LANGUAGE:
Learners demonstrate B1 competence by narrating,
sequencing, and contextualizing personal and fictional
experiences within coherent, task-based story scenarios.

-------------------------------------------------------------
SPIRAL 4 — B2 (Vantage)
-------------------------------------------------------------

CEFR FOCUS:
- Interaction with fluency and spontaneity
- Clear, detailed expression on a wide range of topics
- Understanding implicit meaning and nuance

CEFR CAN-DO ALIGNMENT:
- Can interact with a degree of fluency and spontaneity that makes
  regular interaction with native speakers quite possible
- Can account for and sustain viewpoints
- Can understand the main ideas of complex texts on both concrete
  and abstract topics, including technical discussions in their field
  of specialization

GAME ACTIONS:
- Dialogue with the Living World
- Comparative narration and evaluative decision-making
- Choice-based interaction requiring pragmatic adaptation
- Managing misunderstanding, repair, and clarification

INSTITUTIONAL LANGUAGE:
Learners demonstrate B2 competence by sustaining extended interaction,
adjusting register and tone, interpreting implicit meaning, and
responding appropriately to communicative demands across complex,
multi-step tasks.

-------------------------------------------------------------
SPIRAL 5 — C1 (Effective Operational Proficiency)
-------------------------------------------------------------

CEFR FOCUS:
- Flexible and effective language use
- Expression of abstract and complex ideas
- Nuanced meaning and stylistic control

CEFR CAN-DO ALIGNMENT:
- Can express ideas fluently and spontaneously without much obvious
  searching for expressions
- Can use language flexibly and effectively for social, academic,
  and professional purposes
- Can understand implicit meaning, metaphor, and idiomatic usage
- Can produce clear, well-structured discourse on complex subjects

GAME ACTIONS:
- Symbol Interpretation and metaphorical reasoning
- Abstract dialogue with non-human and human agents
- Intention-driven language choices with layered consequences
- Reformulating meaning across perspectives

INSTITUTIONAL LANGUAGE:
Learners demonstrate C1 competence by interpreting, negotiating,
and producing language with high degrees of abstraction, nuance,
and communicative intention within open-ended, culturally grounded
narrative contexts.

-------------------------------------------------------------
SPIRAL 6 — C2 (Mastery)
-------------------------------------------------------------

CEFR FOCUS:
- Precision, subtlety, and adaptability
- Mediation between perspectives
- Full control of linguistic resources

CEFR CAN-DO ALIGNMENT:
- Can express themselves spontaneously, very fluently, and precisely,
  differentiating finer shades of meaning even in more complex situations
- Can understand virtually everything heard or read
- Can summarize information from different spoken and written sources,
  reconstructing arguments and accounts in a coherent presentation
- Can mediate and reformulate complex discourse for different audiences

GAME ACTIONS:
- Restoring Balance through ethical mediation
- Authoring original myths, narratives, and discourse
- Reframing conflicts using advanced pragmatic and stylistic control
- Bearing responsibility for long-term consequences of language choices

INSTITUTIONAL LANGUAGE:
Learners demonstrate C2 competence by mediating, reframing, and authoring
complex discourse with full control of linguistic, pragmatic, and stylistic
resources in high-responsibility, culturally embedded scenarios.

#####################################################################
# 4. ASSESSMENT & EVIDENCE (INSTITUTIONAL REQUIREMENTS)
#####################################################################

Assessment is embedded in meaningful task performance and is:

- Observable through learner choices and actions
- Traceable across recurring encounters and spirals
- Focused on communicative effectiveness rather than error counting

Evidence collected includes:
- Task completion pathways
- Language choice and reformulation logs
- Repair and mediation strategies
- Adaptation to interlocutor, context, and intention

This assessment model supports:
- Formative and summative evaluation
- Portfolio-based assessment
- CEFR-aligned reporting and certification

#####################################################################
# 5. COMPATIBILITY WITH INSTITUTIONAL USE
#####################################################################

The system is compatible with:

- CEFR-based placement and benchmarking
- Modular curriculum integration
- Blended, guided, and self-directed learning models
- Formal accreditation, auditing, and reporting requirements

CEFR descriptors are:
- Internally tagged to game actions and outcomes
- Externally reportable for institutional documentation
- Mappable to syllabi, learning outcomes, and competency frameworks

#####################################################################
# 6. FINAL ALIGNMENT STATEMENT
#####################################################################

La Aventura de Yaguará aligns with CEFR by:

- Treating learners as social agents engaged in action-oriented tasks
- Scaling communicative competence through depth, recurrence,
  and responsibility
- Embedding assessment within authentic, meaningful language use

The system satisfies institutional standards while preserving
experiential, culturally grounded, non-linear learning.
