## POEMS

### B1 — Short, concrete, democratic voice, accessible grammar

| # | Title | Lines | Theme | Notes |
|---|-------|-------|-------|-------|
| 1 | **I Hear America Singing** | 416–433 | work/identity | 13 lines. A catalogue of workers singing. Carpenter, mason, boatman, shoemaker, mother, girl. Concrete nouns, present participles. The simplest Whitman poem — a list of people being alive. |
| 2 | **O Captain! My Captain!** | 10999–11027 | death/leadership | 24 lines, 3 stanzas. Lincoln elegy. The ONLY Whitman poem with traditional rhyme and meter. "Fallen cold and dead" refrain. Dramatic, emotional, structured. His most famous. |
| 3 | **Beautiful Women** | 9027–9031 | beauty/age | 2 lines. "The young are beautiful — but the old are more beautiful than the young." A couplet. Perfect B1 discussion starter. |
| 4 | **A Farm Picture** | 8999–9003 | nature | 3 lines. A barn, a pasture, cattle and horses feeding. Purest image. |
| 5 | **Cavalry Crossing a Ford** | 9819–9831 | war/image | 7 lines. A snapshot: soldiers crossing a river. Colors (scarlet, blue, white), horses splashing. Pure image poem. |
| 6 | **When I Heard the Learn'd Astronomer** | 8880–8890 | knowledge | 8 lines. Bored by the lecture → goes outside → looks at the stars in silence. Science vs. experience. One of the most taught poems in English. |
| 7 | **O Me! O Life!** | 8903–8918 | meaning | 9 lines + answer. "That the powerful play goes on, and you may contribute a verse." THE Whitman quote. Direct, philosophical, punchy. |

## KEY INSIGHTS

### Whitman's dimension in the English course
Where Poe is the architect, Dickinson the compressor, and Carroll the logician, Whitman is **the expander**. He teaches the long breath, the catalogue, the democratic eye that sees everything as worthy of poetry. His grammar is simple but his *ambition* is cosmic.

### The B1 entry is easier than it looks
Whitman's short poems ("Beautiful Women," "A Farm Picture," "When I Heard the Learn'd Astronomer") are among the simplest in the English canon. The grammar is present tense, the vocabulary concrete. The difficulty comes only when the poems get long.

### Song of Myself as C2 capstone
Song of Myself is too long to read whole (1,346 lines, 52 sections). But selected sections create a powerful arc: the invitation (1–10), the great catalogues (24–33), the farewell (48–52). This mirrors how the Spanish course handles La vorágine — excerpts, not the whole novel.

### War poems as B2 core
Whitman's Drum-Taps cycle is the best war poetry in English — short, imagistic, emotionally devastating. "Vigil Strange" and "A Sight in Camp" are among the most teachable poems at any level. They pair naturally with Poe's horror (both deal in death) but from the opposite direction: Poe aestheticizes death, Whitman humanizes it.

### Crossing Brooklyn Ferry = speaking to the student
"Others will see the islands large and small; / Fifty years hence, others will see them as they cross..." — Whitman is literally speaking to the language learner across time. This is the most meta-pedagogical poem in the course.
