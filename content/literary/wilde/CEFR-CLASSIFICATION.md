## KEY INSIGHT
Wilde is the **paradox** author. Where Austen gives irony (saying one thing, meaning another), Wilde gives **paradox** (saying two opposite things and meaning both). "The truth is rarely pure and never simple" is itself pure and simple. The surface is B2 (short sentences, everyday dialogue) but the meaning is C1 (every line inverts expectation). Lady Bracknell's interview is the single most teachable comic scene in English drama.
