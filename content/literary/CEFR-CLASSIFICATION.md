## KEY INSIGHTS

1. **A1 gap**: Only 6 strong literary peaks for 8 destinations. Yaguará fills dest 7-8.
2. **A2-B1 bridge is the richest zone**: 10 passages from 4 authors — creative weaving matters most here.
3. **Carrasquilla = B1 floor**: No A2 material. Antioqueño dialect makes everything B1-B2 minimum.
4. **Obeso dual-track**: Every poem exists as dialect + castizo. Use castizo at B1, paired at B2, dialect-only at C1.
5. **Macías spans the entire range**: A1 (Rana ch.1) through C2 (Las muertes de Jung). He IS the backbone.
6. **Whitman adds international dimension**: Valentina's translations bridge the Spanish and future English courses.
7. **Unclassified material is vast**: 10 Carrasquilla stories, 24 Macías short stories, 8 Mejía poems, 13 Obeso poems, 4 Acosta works. Future sessions should classify these to deepen the pool.
8. **Material classifies itself**: Don't force authors into levels. The language dictates the CEFR, not our preconceptions.
