## KEY INSIGHT
Twain is the **vernacular** author. Where Austen gives polished irony and Wilde gives polished paradox, Twain gives **truth spoken in dialect**. The challenge for learners is that the language looks "wrong" — misspellings, double negatives, non-standard grammar — but the moral vision is the most advanced in the entire English course. Jim's "Trash" speech at C1 and "I'll go to hell" at C2 are the ethical peaks of the program. Twain teaches students that **how someone speaks has nothing to do with how deeply they think**.
