## WHOLE-TEXT CLASSIFICATION: C1–C2

This entire story operates at C1–C2. There are no B1 or B2 sections. The prose is a single stream of consciousness: constant shifts between exterior observation and interior memory, sentences that drift across time and space without warning.

At 310 lines, it is short enough to assign as a complete text — the student's first encounter with full stream of consciousness.

## RECOMMENDED USE

**C1**: Opening (lines 56–73) as introduction to stream of consciousness technique
**C2**: Full story as capstone modernist text — pairs with Dickinson (compression) and Poe (interiority)

## KEY INSIGHT
Woolf is the **modernist** author. Where Austen gives social surfaces, Woolf gives **what happens underneath a woman buying gloves**. The entire story is one walk, one purchase, one hour — but inside that hour is WWI, Shelley, Shakespeare, childhood, faith, death, and the shopgirl's holiday at Brighton. This is what C2 reading looks like: everything happening at once.
