## 1. EL SUPERHÉROE CAPA DURA (1,517 lines)
**"Una aventura en el caballo del tiempo"**
Dedicated to Emmanuel, Miguel and Raquel (David and María Angélica's children, bisnietos de Rafael).
Written 1991–2021 (30 years!).

### Structure
39 chapters. David (8 years old) gets a red cape from his grandmother → becomes DSHCD (David, el Superhéroe Capa Dura) → rides the "caballo del tiempo" through literary/historical worlds → solves riddles from the "fuente del conocimiento" → meets Shakespeare, Cervantes, Dante, Borges, Andersen, Wilde, Carroll, Marco Polo → returns home having forgotten the adventure.

### CEFR Classification by Chapter Groups

| Chapters | Lines | CEFR | Content | Notes |
|----------|-------|------|---------|-------|
| I–III | 1–130 | **A2** | David wants a cape, buys fabric in Rionegro, imagines flying | Present/past tense, concrete vocabulary, dialogue-driven. Purest A2 Macías. |
| IV–V | 131–198 | **A2-B1** | Investiture scene, Don Quixote reading, naming ceremony | "Armado caballero." The reading of Don Quixote = metafiction begins. |
| VI–IX | 199–410 | **B1** | Bamboo forest, the elf, Jack and the Beanstalk retold, the horse of time | Embedded fairy tale (Jack/habichuelas). Quest structure. Vocabulary stretches. |
| X–XIV | 411–600 | **B1-B2** | Desert, "Parábola de la muerte a la espera," Bagdad, Marco Polo, Greco the dog | Father's embedded stories. Time layering (abuelo→padre→hijo). Philosophical depth increases. |
| XV–XXII | 600–720 | **B2** | Don Quixote's cave, the black hole, the riddles begin, cerro Pandeazúcar | Literary allusions (Cervantes, Montesinos). Riddle quest structure. |
| XXIII–XXIX | 720–1000 | **B2-C1** | Two Davids meet, the font of knowledge, Shakespeare in the tavern, 9 riddles | Shakespeare writing Hamlet (line 1005!). Dante/Virgilio/Borges assembly. Time philosophy. |
| XXX–XXXVI | 1000–1390 | **C1** | David's future family, poets' assembly, Andersen at the dock, Matías riddle | Macías's own children appear. "El eco y el caracol" (Andersen's song). The keystone riddle! |
| XXXVII–XXXIX | 1390–1517 | **B1-B2** | Matías solves the final riddle, the horse dances, David returns | "Ayer con una, ahora con dos, mañana con tres" → LA A. Resolution. Return to bamboo forest. |

### CRITICAL CONNECTION
This novel IS the Spanish course in narrative form. It contains:
- The keystone riddle (La A) — Ch. XXXVII
- Don Ernesto and the fuente del conocimiento — same as La Rana sin Dientes
- The embedded parables — same technique as all Macías narrative
- Every major literary author we use in the English course appears AS A CHARACTER

## KEY INSIGHTS

### Superhéroe IS the meta-narrative
This novel connects EVERYTHING:
- The keystone riddle (La A) appears in Ch. XXXVII
- Don Ernesto and the fuente del conocimiento = same as La Rana sin Dientes
- Shakespeare, Cervantes, Dante, Borges, Wilde, Carroll, Andersen ALL appear as characters
- The "caballo del tiempo" = the course itself — riding through literature across time

### El hombre universal is the C1-C2 microfiction collection
60 stories, most under 20 lines. Borges-like compression. Several overlap with Cuentos infantiles (picture-book versions). "Hallazgo de unos huesos" also appears embedded in Superhéroe Ch. XII — the same story told three times, at three levels.

### The translation candidates create a BRIDGE
Macías in English doesn't replace Poe or Dickinson — it CONNECTS to them. "El clan de los libros" speaks to Dickinson's "A word is dead." "Hallazgo de unos huesos" speaks to Hamlet's Yorick. The student who reads Macías in Spanish and then in English sees the same ideas reflected across languages. That IS interlinguistics.
