# Macías Riddles — English Translations for A1-A2
# Source: "Quien no la adivina bien tonto es..." (100 riddles, 2004/2020)
# Translated for The Night Garden English course
# Original: Luis Fernando Macías | Translations: Valentina Macías Isaza
#
# Selection criteria: concrete vocabulary, guessable from imagery,
# animals/nature/body/household — topics that work at A1-A2
#
# TRANSLATION PRINCIPLES:
# 1. The answer must be guessable from the English alone
# 2. Rhythm over exact rhyme — Macías uses cuartetos de arte menor
# 3. No filler words just for rhyme — every word must serve the riddle
# 4. Keep the compression — 4 lines is the target, never more than 5

## A1 RIDDLES (15) — Present simple, basic vocabulary, very concrete

### R-A1-01 | Answer: The sun (El sol) | Original #16
**Spanish:**
Sin ser charol / reluce y brilla, / calienta mucho / sin ser parrilla.

**English:**
Not polished nor painted,
Yet how it shines!
It warms the world
without a fire's lines.

---

### R-A1-02 | Answer: The eyes (Los ojos) | Original #56
**Spanish:**
Las dos, ventanas; / o las dos, puertas; / sólo has de verlas / si están abiertas.

**English:**
The two windows
Or the two doors
You only see through them
When they're not closed.

---

### R-A1-03 | Answer: The tongue (La lengua) | Original #21
**Spanish:**
Entra y sale, / sube y baja, / la prisionera en / la misma caja.

**English:**
In and out
Up and down
A prisoner trapped
In the same old town.

---

### R-A1-04 | Answer: The shadow (La sombra) | Original #28
**Spanish:**
Todos la tienen, / a nadie ocupa; / a todos sigue / sin ser su culpa.

**English:**
Everyone has one
It bothers none
Follows you always
Bound to come.

### R-A1-05 | Answer: The canary (El canario) | Original #4
**Spanish:**
Yema de huevo, / pico ligero: / bonito el canto / del prisionero.

**English:**
Yellow as egg yolk
With a tiny beak
How sweet the songs
Of the prisoner who sings!

### R-A1-06 | Answer: The butterfly (La mariposa) | Original #5

**English:**

not a book nor grass:
with brand-new wings,
The worm flies at last.

of tiny rice,

## KEYSTONE RIDDLE — English Course
# Answer: A word

**The riddle:**
What is dead when it is said,
yet begins to live that day?

**Answer: A word.**

**When solved, the full poem appears:**

> A word is dead
> When it is said,
> Some say.
> I say it just
> Begins to live
> That day.
>
> — Emily Dickinson, *Poems, Third Series*

**The arc across both courses:**

| | El Viaje del Jaguar (Spanish) | The Night Garden (English) |
|--|-------------------------------|---------------------------|
| Quest | Search for the lost name | Bring the word to life |
| Keystone by | A child (Matías Macías, age 8) | A poet (Emily Dickinson) |
| Answer | The letter A | A word |
| Meaning | The seed | What grows |

A letter is dead until it becomes a word.
A word is dead until someone says it.
The student said it. It lives.
